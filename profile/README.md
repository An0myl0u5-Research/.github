We are a decentralised research collective building infrastructure for **community‑verified knowledge**, cryptographically signed and permanently attributed. Every change — from a one‑word correction to a full research corpus — is treated like code: versioned, auditable, and optionally GPG‑signed for provenance. [page:1]

> An0m5‑ARC — the **An0myl0u5 Research Collective** — is the open research layer of the wider An0myl0u5 network (personal blockchains, AI data, and transaction histories), maintained independently of the private An0myl0u5‑Research venture.

## Who can contribute

Anyone. Literally anyone.

- A nurse who spots an error in a clinical paper and wants it corrected on‑chain‑style, not lost in email. [page:1]  
- A developer who wants to sign their annotations with **cryptographic authority**. [page:1]  
- An academic who wants corrections and commentary recorded as part of their scholarly output. [page:1]  
- Someone who prefers anonymity but still wants a **verifiable trace** via keys, DIDs, or attestations. [page:1]  

**You don’t need to know GitHub.** A submission portal abstracts Git, branches, and PRs into simple “submit + sign” flows. [page:1]

---

## How attribution works

Every commit, annotation, and submission in this organisation is:

1. Timestamped. [page:1]  
2. Linked to a contributor identifier (GitHub, ORCID, DID, or local ARC ID). [page:1]  
3. Permanently recorded in Git history. [page:1]  
4. Optionally **GPG‑signed** for cryptographic proof of authorship. [page:1]  

Think of it as **academic citation meets code signing**: papers, notes, reviews, and counter‑arguments become first‑class Git objects with verifiable provenance.

## Blockchain, identity, and cryptography

### Blockchain and storage

- Ethereum, Bitcoin, EVM‑compatible chains — on‑chain anchoring, payments, and governance‑adjacent experiments.  
- IPFS — decentralised, content‑addressed storage for research artefacts, proofs, and large datasets.

### Cryptography

- GPG signing — default mechanism for signing commits and annotations with strong identity guarantees. [page:1]  
- Post‑quantum cryptography — integrated (via the An0myl0u5 Ωm3gA wallet) for **future‑proof** contribution security.  
- Zero‑knowledge proofs — ZK voting and verifiable tallying for governance and sensitive research contexts.

### Identity and governance

- Decentralised Identity (DID), ORCID, Trust Registry Protocol (Trust over IP) — layered identity, from academic IDs to decentralised identifiers.  
- GitVote (CNCF), 1‑person‑1‑vote attestations, ZK‑secured ballots — **transparent but privacy‑respecting** decision‑making.  
- Git‑based attribution protocol — timestamped, ID‑linked, signed commits as the canonical source of truth.
