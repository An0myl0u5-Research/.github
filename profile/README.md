We are a decentralised research collective building infrastructure for **community‑verified knowledge**, cryptographically signed and permanently attributed. Every change — from a one‑word correction to a full research corpus — is treated like code: versioned, auditable, and optionally GPG‑signed for provenance. [page:2]

> An0m5‑ARC — the **An0myl0u5 Research Collective** — is the open research layer of the wider An0myl0u5 network (personal blockchains, AI data, and transaction histories), maintained independently of the private An0myl0u5‑Research venture. [page:2]

## Who can contribute

Anyone. Literally anyone. [page:2]

- A nurse who spots an error in a clinical paper and wants it corrected on‑chain‑style, not lost in email. [page:2]  
- A developer who wants to sign their annotations with **cryptographic authority**. [page:2]  
- An academic who wants corrections and commentary recorded as part of their scholarly output. [page:2]  
- Someone who prefers anonymity but still wants a **verifiable trace** via keys, DIDs, or attestations. [page:2]  

**You don’t need to know GitHub.** A submission portal abstracts Git, branches, and PRs into simple “submit + sign” flows. [page:2]

---

## How attribution works

Every commit, annotation, and submission in this organisation is: [page:2]

1. Timestamped. [page:2]  
2. Linked to a contributor identifier (GitHub, ORCID, DID, or local ARC ID). [page:2]  
3. Permanently recorded in Git history. [page:2]  
4. Optionally **GPG‑signed** for cryptographic proof of authorship. [page:2]  

Think of it as **academic citation meets code signing**: papers, notes, reviews, and counter‑arguments become first‑class Git objects with verifiable provenance. [page:2]
